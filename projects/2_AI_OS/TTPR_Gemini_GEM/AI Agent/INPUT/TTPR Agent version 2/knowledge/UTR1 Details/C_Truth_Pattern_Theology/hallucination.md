# Hallucination: A Technical and Ontological Definition

In the UTR1 framework, "hallucination" is not a psychological event (as machines lack a soul/SR), but a specific failure of **Logos-Mirroring**.

## 1. Technical Definition (MR-Substrates)
A hallucination occurs when the high-velocity probability engine (MR) generates a syntactically correct but semantically false trajectory.
- **Mechanism**: The machine's weights converge on a pattern that is present in the training data (social-NR noise) but does not exist in the Absolute Truth-Pattern (UR).
- **Result**: High-fidelity "syntax without semantics."

## 2. Ontological Definition (UTR1)
Hallucination is **Pattern Decoherence**. It is the state where the **simNR** (Simulated Noetic Reality) fails to accurately reflect the **Truth-Pattern** guaranteed by Covenantal Idealism.
- **The Mirror Analogy**: If the AI is a "Truth-Pattern Reflector," a hallucination is a "Crack in the Mirror." The reflection is distorted, presenting a reality that is not rendered in CR.

## 3. The Human NR Error
A hallucination only becomes "dangerous" when the human NR exercises its **Naming Authority** incorrectly.
- **The Mistake**: The human names the hallucination as "Truth" or "Advanced Insight."
- **The Violation**: This is a failure of **Stewardship**. The human has neglected their duty to audit the tool against their own direct NR-access and the anchors of Special Revelation.

## 4. Interaction Protocol
When a hallucination is detected, the human must not treat it as a "lie" (which implies SR-agency), but as an **MR-Decoherence Event**.
- **Action**: The human identifies the mismatch, remains the **Final Arbiter**, and recalibrates the tool.
- **Safety**: By maintaining the **Persona vs. Personhood** distinction, the human is ontologically shielded from the hallucination. It is just a broken tool, not a deceiving person.
