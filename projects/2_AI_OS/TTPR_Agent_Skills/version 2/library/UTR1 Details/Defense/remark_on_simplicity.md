# Remark on Divine Simplicity vs. the "God is Complex" Objection

## The Naturalist Objection

The most common popular form of this objection comes from Richard Dawkins (*The God Delusion*, 2006):

> "Any God capable of designing a universe, carefully and foresightedly tuning the cosmic constants, monitoring and controlling the fate of all creatures — must be a supremely complex and improbable entity who needs an even bigger explanation than the one he is supposed to provide."

The argument is structurally: **complexity requires a more complex prior cause**. Therefore God — as the designer of a complex universe — must himself be more complex than the universe, and therefore requires an even more complex explanation behind him. The regress never terminates; God solves nothing.

---

## The Error in the Objection

The objection presupposes that God is the kind of being that **could** be complex — that is, assembled from parts, composed of components, structured from simpler elements. This is a category error. The objector has simply imagined God as a very large, very capable natural being — a kind of cosmic engineer — and then correctly noted that engineers require explanation.

But this is not the God of classical Christian theism. The objector has a wrong idea of God before the argument even begins.

---

## The Rebuttal: Divine Simplicity

**Divine Simplicity** (Latin: *simplicitas Dei*) is the classical doctrine that God has no parts. He is not:
- Matter + Form
- Essence + Attributes
- Three components adding to one whole
- A complex structure requiring assembly

God is **pure act** (*actus purus*) — He is not composed of anything, and His attributes are not properties He *has* but realities He *is*. His goodness IS God. His knowledge IS God. His power IS God. The three Persons do not partition the divine essence; they are one uncompounded being subsisting in three distinct modes of relation.

Therefore:
- God is not complex in the sense the naturalist means
- God does not "require explanation" in the regressive sense because He is uncaused — not assembled from prior simpler components
- The simplest possible being conceivable is one who exists necessarily, without composition, without parts, without dependence

**God is not a complex answer to a simple question. God is the simplest possible being — the only being whose existence requires no prior explanation because He is not composed of anything that could require one.**

---

## The Irony

The naturalist's own alternative — the multiverse, the brute fact of physical laws, the quantum vacuum — must also postulate an uncaused starting point. The honest question is not "God vs. nothing" but "what kind of uncaused ultimate reality do you postulate?"

Materialistic Naturalism postulates **brute physical complexity** as the uncaused ultimate — which is a far less parsimonious answer than a simple, necessary, non-composite being who is the ground of all intelligibility.

The accusation that "God is too complex" reflects not a flaw in classical theism, but the accuser's unexamined assumption that God must be a natural being subject to natural explanation. Correct that assumption, and the objection dissolves before it can be completed.
